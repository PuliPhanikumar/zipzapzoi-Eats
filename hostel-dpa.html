Data Processing Agreement

Between You ("Data Principal") and ZipZapZoi Technologies Pvt. Ltd. ("Data Fiduciary")

1. Scope and Purpose

This Data Processing Agreement ("DPA") governs the processing of personal data by the Data Fiduciary on behalf of the Data Principal in connection with the Zoi Hostel POS service. This DPA is compliant with the Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023 (DPDP Act).

2. Definitions

  • Data Principal: The hostel owner/manager who controls the data entered into the platform.
  • Data Fiduciary: ZipZapZoi Technologies Pvt. Ltd., which processes data on behalf of the Data Principal.
  • Personal Data: Any data about an individual who is identifiable, including hosteler names, Aadhaar numbers, contact details, and financial records.
  • Significant Data Fiduciary: If applicable based on volume and sensitivity of data processed.

3. Categories of Personal Data Processed

Category Data Elements Purpose Retention
Identity Data Name, Aadhaar, PAN, photo KYC compliance Active + 90 days
Contact Data Phone, email, address Communication Active + 90 days
Financial Data Rent, payments, invoices Billing & GST 7 years (tax law)
Accommodation Data Room, bed, check-in/out Operations Active + 90 days
Health Data Blood group, emergency contact Safety Active + 90 days

4. Lawful Basis for Processing

Processing is carried out based on:

  • Consent: Hostelers provide consent during admission (Aadhaar upload, personal details).
  • Contractual Necessity: Processing required to fulfill the hostel accommodation contract.
  • Legal Obligation: GST compliance, tax record retention, and government reporting.
  • Legitimate Interest: AI analytics for operational improvement (anonymized data only).

5. Data Security Measures

  • AES-256 encryption for stored data.
  • TLS 1.3 for all data transmission.
  • Role-based access control (Owner/Warden roles).
  • Multi-factor authentication for Owner accounts.
  • Regular security audits and vulnerability assessments.
  • All servers located within India.

6. Sub-Processors

The Data Fiduciary may engage sub-processors for:

  • Cloud hosting (Indian data centers only).
  • Payment processing (PCI-DSS compliant gateways).
  • SMS/Email OTP delivery.

All sub-processors are bound by equivalent data protection obligations.

7. Data Principal Rights

As per the DPDP Act 2023, Data Principals (hostelers and hostel owners) have the right to:

  • Access their personal data.
  • Correct inaccurate or misleading data.
  • Erase data (subject to legal retention).
  • Nominate another individual to exercise rights.
  • File grievances with the Data Protection Board of India.

8. Data Breach Notification

In the event of a personal data breach, the Data Fiduciary will:

  • Notify the Data Protection Board of India within 72 hours.
  • Notify affected Data Principals without undue delay.
  • Take immediate remediation measures.
  • Maintain a breach register with details and actions taken.

9. Cross-Border Data Transfer

Personal data is not transferred outside India. All processing occurs on servers located within the territory of India, in compliance with Section 16 of the DPDP Act.

10. Audit & Compliance

The Data Principal may request annual audit reports on data processing activities. The Data Fiduciary will cooperate with any audits conducted by or on behalf of the Data Protection Board of India.

11. Termination & Data Return

Upon termination of the subscription, the Data Fiduciary will:

  • Provide a 30-day data export window.
  • Delete all personal data within 90 days of termination (except records required by law).
  • Provide written confirmation of deletion upon request.

12. Governing Law

This DPA is governed by the laws of India, specifically the DPDP Act 2023 and the Information Technology Act, 2000. Disputes are subject to the jurisdiction of courts in Bengaluru, Karnataka.
